# CARE-KV before/after 3D distribution figures (Phase K-d)

Per-layer 3D `(Channel × Token × |value|)` surfaces showing the K and V
distributions in three regimes — fp16, INT3 base_quant, and INT3 CARE-KV
(base_quant + selected stored residuals).

## Setup

- **Model**: `TinyLlama/TinyLlama-1.1B-Chat-v1.0`  (22 layers, 4 kv-heads, head_dim=64)
- **Sequence length**: 512 tokens (pangram repeat prompt)
- **Channel axis**: flattened `num_kv_heads × head_dim = 4 × 64 = 256`
- **Quantization**: INT3, symmetric per-group, `group_size = 32`
  (= `k_channel_group`)
- **Store-time selection** (paper-best config, simulated standalone):
  - `page_size = 16` tokens, `STORE_ABS_K = 2`, `STORE_ABS_V = 4`
  - K: top-2 tokens per (page, kv_head, channel_group of 32 channels) by
    `‖R_K_segment‖₂`; **stored fraction = 12.5%**
  - V: top-4 tokens per (page, kv_head) by `‖R_V_token‖₂`;
    **stored fraction = 25.0%**
  - `K_care_store = K_hat + R_K · mask_K`,
    `V_care_store = V_hat + R_V · mask_V`
- **z axis**: `|value|`, with **shared z-scale per row** (K row uses
  `max(|K_fp|, |K_hat|, |K_care|)`, V row uses the analogous V max) so the
  three panels in each row are visually comparable.

## Interpretation

1. **CARE-KV measurably reduces reconstruction error vs INT3 base_quant
   at every layer**, with V improving roughly 2× more than K in relative
   terms (~10% K RMSE reduction, ~22% V RMSE reduction).

2. **V improves more than K despite both using only stored residuals.**
   Two reasons visible in the data:
   - V's outliers concentrate on a *handful of tokens* (one stored token
     pulls a full 64-dim residual vector back to fp16). K's outliers
     concentrate on *channels*, so storing top-2 tokens per channel-group
     leaves most channel-group cells uncorrected.
   - The V budget is 2× the K budget (25% vs 12.5%) under the paper-best
     `STORE_ABS_K=2, STORE_ABS_V=4` setting — i.e., V gets more slots per
     page per kv_head.

3. **Outlier max magnitudes are preserved across all three regimes** at
   every layer (e.g., layer 21: |K_fp|max = |K_hat|max = |K_care|max =
   19.66). INT3 round-trip preserves outlier peaks because the per-group
   scale absorbs them; CARE-KV's gain shows up in the *noise floor* and
   *mid-range cells*, not the headline peaks. This is consistent with
   the visual subtlety of the before/after surfaces — the differences
   are in the small-magnitude residual structure, not the dominant
   ridges.

4. **Magnitude growth with depth (already noted in `activation_3d_figures.md`)
   is mirrored in absolute RMSE.** K RMSE grows 2.4× from layer 0 to 21
   (0.41 → 1.00); V RMSE grows 40× (0.003 → 0.137). The *relative*
   improvement from CARE-KV stays in the 9–23% range across depths,
   which is why CARE-KV's PPL gain compounds across the full network.

## What this figure does *not* show

- Decode-time read budget (`READ_ABS_K=2, READ_ABS_V=2`) is not applied —
  these surfaces show the *store-side* upper bound (what CARE-KV *could*
  recover if every stored slot were read). The router's read budget at
  decode further filters this.
- The K-residual correction term `ΔO_K ≈ Σ a · (q·R_K) · (V − O_base)` is
  a first-order Jacobian approximation, not direct K substitution — these
  panels show the post-substitution K values, which is the upper-bound
  view of "what CARE-KV recovers."
- Single prompt — outlier-channel identity is roughly prompt-invariant
  in our spot checks but a multi-prompt average would strengthen the claim.
