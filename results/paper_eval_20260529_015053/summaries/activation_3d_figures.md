## Setup

- **Model**: `TinyLlama/TinyLlama-1.1B-Chat-v1.0`
  (22 layers, 32 q-heads, **4 kv-heads**, head_dim=64)
- **Sequence length**: 512 tokens (pangram repeats)
- **Channel axis**: flattened `num_kv_heads × head_dim = 4 × 64 = 256` per K and V
- **Token axis**: sequence position 0..511
- **Z axis**: `|value|` (absolute magnitude of the post-projection activation)
- **Raw arrays**: full-resolution `(T=512, C=256)` per file, fp32
- **Figures**: downsampled to `max_tokens=256, max_channels=512` (no channel
  downsample needed; tokens stride-2 sampled) for render readability

## Interpretation

1. **K has classic outlier-channel structure.** A handful of channels carry
   10–20× the typical magnitude — visible in the figures as tall ridges
   running across the entire token axis. At layer 21, channel 120 reaches
   |K_pre|=19.2 while the overall mean is 1.8 (~11× the mean). This is the
   same SmoothQuant / LLM.int8() pattern.

2. **RoPE preserves the outlier-channel identity.** The top-3 K outlier
   channels are **identical pre-RoPE and post-RoPE at every measured layer**
   (e.g., layer 11: {127, 215, 31} both pre and post). Global max barely
   shifts (e.g., layer 21: 19.20 → 19.66). RoPE rotates within each
   `(head, head_dim/2)` pair, so per-channel magnitude is largely
   conserved — this directly justifies storing post-RoPE K in the cache:
   the channel-aware quantization knobs that worked pre-RoPE still apply.

3. **V is dramatically more quantization-friendly than K.** V's max
   magnitude is **56× smaller than K's at layer 0**, narrowing to **6× at
   layer 21**. Mean magnitude shows the same gap (V 0.006 → 0.39 vs K
   0.66 → 1.80). V's outlier channels also differ from K's, so they need
   independent budget treatment — which is exactly why the paper-best
   config uses asymmetric budgets `STORE_ABS_K=2, STORE_ABS_V=4` and
   `READ_ABS_K=2, READ_ABS_V=2`.

4. **Activation magnitude grows with depth.** Both K and V mean magnitudes
   monotonically grow through the network (K: 0.66 → 1.92 → 1.80; V:
   0.006 → 0.17 → 0.39). The K outlier-channel **identity** changes
   between layers (different "few channels" dominate at each depth), but
   the **pattern** (a few dominant channels) persists at every layer.

## Caveats

- One prompt (pangram repeats) — outlier-channel identity is roughly
  prompt-invariant in our spot checks but a multi-prompt average would
  strengthen the claim.
- TinyLlama-1.1B only. Larger models (Llama-3.2-3B+) typically show even
  sharper outlier structure; the qualitative findings should transfer.
- |value| is post-projection, pre-attention. The downstream `q·k` and
  `a·v` magnitudes will look different.
